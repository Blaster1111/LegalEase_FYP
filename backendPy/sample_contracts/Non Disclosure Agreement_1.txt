IN CONNECTION WITH THE ABOVE, THE PARTIES HEREBY AGREE AS FOLLOWS:

1.  “Confidential and or proprietary Information” shall mean and include
    any information disclosed by one Party (Disclosing Party) to the
    other (Receiving Party) either directly or indirectly, in writing,
    orally, by inspection of tangible objects (including, without
    limitation, documents, prototypes, samples, media, documentation,
    discs and code). Confidential information shall include, without
    limitation, any materials, trade secrets, network information,
    configurations, trademarks, brand name, know-how, business and
    marketing plans, financial and operational information, and all
    other non-public information, material or data relating to the
    current and/or future business and operations of the Disclosing
    Party and analysis, compilations, studies, summaries, extracts or
    other documentation prepared by the Disclosing Party. Confidential
    Information may also include information disclosed to the Receiving
    Party by third parties on behalf of the Disclosing Party.

2.  The Receiving Party shall refrain from disclosing, reproducing,
    summarising and/or distributing Confidential Information and
    confidential materials of the Disclosing Party except in connection
    with the Proposed Transaction.

3.  The Parties shall protect the confidentiality of each other’s
    Confidential Information in the same manner as they protect the
    confidentiality of their own proprietary and confidential
    information of similar nature.

4.  Confidential Information shall at all times remain the property of
    the Disclosing Party and may not be copied or reproduced by the
    Receiving Party without the Disclosing Party’s prior written
    consent.

5.  Within seven (7) days of a written request by the Disclosing Party,
    the Receiving Party shall return/destroy all originals, copies,
    reproductions and summaries of Confidential Information.

6.  The Receiving Party may disclose the Confidential Information only
    to employees and consultants on a need-to-know basis.

7.  Confidential Information shall not include information that:

i)  is public without breach of this Agreement;
ii) was already in possession of the Receiving Party;
iii) was independently developed;
iv) was approved for release in writing.

8.  If disclosure is required by law, the Receiving Party shall notify
    the Disclosing Party and cooperate to limit disclosure.

9.  Neither Party shall use the other’s name, trademarks or symbols
    without prior written approval.

10. Each Party agrees that breach may cause irreparable injury and the
    other Party may seek injunctive relief.

11. The Receiving Party shall indemnify the Disclosing Party for costs
    and damages arising from breach.

12. Neither Party shall be liable for special or consequential damages.

13. Neither Party is obligated to disclose all Confidential Information.

14. This Agreement shall be effective from execution and continue until
    the Proposed Transaction is terminated by thirty (30) days’ notice.
    Confidentiality obligations survive for five (5) years after
    termination.

15. Each Party warrants authority to enter this Agreement.

16. If any provision is invalid, the remainder shall remain enforceable.

17. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts.

18. The relationship between the Parties is principal-to-principal.

19. This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of India. Courts in
    Bangalore shall have jurisdiction. Disputes shall be settled by
    arbitration in Bangalore under the Arbitration & Conciliation Act,
    1996.

20. Additional oral agreements do not exist. Amendments must be in
    writing.

21. This Agreement or rights under it cannot be assigned without written
    consent.
